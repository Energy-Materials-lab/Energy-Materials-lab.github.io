## Solid State Physics
-----
Undergraduate core course in the Department of Physics.  
Covers crystal structures, electronic band theory, semiconductors, and applications in energy materials.  
- Offered regularly in Fall and Spring semesters.  
- Classroom: Physics Building, AUC New Cairo Campus.  

## Nanomaterials for Energy Applications
-----
Graduate course in the Nanotechnology program.  
Focuses on synthesis, characterization, and applications of nanostructured materials in solar fuels, batteries, and supercapacitors.  
- Includes laboratory modules and computational modeling.  

## Renewable Energy Materials and Devices
-----
Undergraduate/graduate elective.  
Introduces solar cells, fuel cells, photocatalysis, and energy storage technologies.  
- Emphasis on sustainable energy solutions and device fabrication.

## Notes
Prof. Allam integrates his teaching with ongoing research at the **Energy Materials Laboratory (EML)**, giving students exposure to both experimental and computational methods. His courses are designed to prepare students for careers in **nanotechnology, energy conversion, and sustainable materials science**.
